Permutation Matrix

A permutation matrix is a square matrix. It’s always raw equivalent to the Identity matrix

For size nnn there exists n!n!n! permutation matrcies.

For example there exists 222 permutation matrcies of size 222 which are:

[1001],[0110] \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} , \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}[10​01​],[01​10​]

A permutation matrix is non-singular, and the determinant is always ±1 \pm 1±1

You can imagine the 1’s in the n×nn \times nn×n matrix as non attacking rooks in an n×n n \times nn×n chessboard

Permutation In Matrix Matrix Multiplication

Left Multiplication

Left Multiplication by a permutation matrix rearranges the corresponding rows

[010001100][x1x2x3]=[x3x1x2] \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x1 \\ x2 \\ x3 \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} x3 \\ x1 \\ x2 \\ \end{bmatrix} ⎣⎡​001​100​010​⎦⎤​⎣⎡​x1x2x3​⎦⎤​=⎣⎡​x3x1x2​⎦⎤​
The 111 in the first row is in the 2nd2^{nd}2nd column so the 2nd2^{nd}2nd the row of the other matrix will be placed in the first row and so on ..
[010001100][aaabbbccc]=[bbbcccaaa] \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} a & a & a \\ b & b & b \\ c & c & c \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} b & b & b \\ c & c & c \\ a & a & a \\ \end{bmatrix} ⎣⎡​001​100​010​⎦⎤​⎣⎡​abc​abc​abc​⎦⎤​=⎣⎡​bca​bca​bca​⎦⎤​

Right Multiplication

Right multiplication by a permutation matrix rearranges the corresponding columns

[abcabcabc][010001100][cabcabcab] \begin{bmatrix} a & b & c \\ a & b & c \\ a & b & c \\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} c & a & b \\ c & a & b \\ c & a & b \\ \end{bmatrix} ⎣⎡​aaa​bbb​ccc​⎦⎤​⎣⎡​001​100​010​⎦⎤​⎣⎡​ccc​aaa​bbb​⎦⎤​
